## 7. Advanced Reasoning: From Chain-of-Thought to Tree-of-Thought

### Evolution of Prompting Methodologies

**Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Limitations**
- Linear reasoning paths unable to explore alternative solutions
- Difficulty with problems requiring backtracking or revision
- Limited ability to handle problems with multiple valid approaches
- Inefficient for complex multi-step reasoning tasks

### Tree-of-Thought (ToT) Architecture

**Core Concept**
Tree-of-thought prompting enables LLMs to explore multiple reasoning paths simultaneously, evaluating each path and selecting the most promising approach before proceeding.

**Technical Implementation**
- Parallel generation of multiple reasoning hypotheses
- Evaluation functions assessing the viability of each path
- Backtracking capabilities when paths prove unproductive
- Beam search algorithms for efficient exploration of reasoning space

### Performance Improvements

**Mathematics and Logic**
- 45% improvement on competition-level mathematical problems
- Near-perfect performance on standard logic puzzles
- Enhanced ability to solve novel problem types not seen in training
- Improved proof generation and verification capabilities
